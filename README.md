# HR Attrition - Employee Retention & Workforce Optimization
Real-world Data cleaning, analysis, and visualization of HR Attrition Data using Microsoft Excel.

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition and workforce stability using **Microsoft Excel**.
The objective is to help HR leadership understand **who is leaving, why they are leaving, and where retention risks exist**, using data-driven insights.

The analysis focuses on employee **demographics, tenure, performance, compensation, employees, and departments** to support better workforce planning and retention strategies.


---


## 🎯 Business Problem
The organization is experiencing workforce attrition due to:
- Voluntary resignations
- Involuntary terminations
- Retirements

HR requires insights to:
- Improve employee retention
- Optimize staffing decisions
- Enhance employee satisfaction
- Identify departmental risks

---

## 🧾 Dataset Description
The dataset (`Employee Data.xlsx`) contains employee-level records including:

- Employee ID
- Gender, Age, Race
- Department, Business Unit, Job Title
- Employee Classification (Full-Time, Part-Time, Contract, Temporary)
- Hire Date & Termination Date
- Termination Type & Reason
- Supervisor
- Pay Zone
- Performance Score

> ⚠️ All data is anonymized and used strictly for analytical practice.

## 🔍 Key Analysis Areas

### 1️⃣ Executive Overview
- Total employees, total active employees, total attrition rate, and average employee age
- Overall Attrition rate across job title, Department, Race, Employment Type, Termination Type, Marital Status, Gender, Age Group, and Payzone

### 2️⃣ Departmental Insights
- Average Rating by Department
- Departmental Attrition by Gender, Average Age, Employee Type, and State
- Average Tenure (In Months) Per Department

### 3️⃣ Employee Analysis
- Relationship between Performance, Payzone and Average Rating
- Average Tenure (In Months) Per Employee Type, Termination Type, Performance Score, and Age Group
- Average Rating Per Employee Type

---

## 📌 Key Insights (Summary)
- Attrition is highest among employees that are married 
- Production department and associated job titles experience consistently higher turnover
- Lower pay zones show higher attrition rates
- Attrition is highest in Massachusetts (Accounting for almost 90% of attrition)
- Temporary and Part-time employees tend to stay for a short period

---

## 💡 Recommendations
- Strengthen onboarding and early-career support
- Review compensation structures in high-turnover pay zones
- Improve workload distribution and performance management
- Improve compensation structures and reduce workload on high-turnover departments
- Enhance employee engagement and recognition programs
- Improve working conditions, and compensation in Massachusetts
